Background

FRONTLINE's early alerts are investigated about the effect of Facebook in the United States and around the world on data protection and democracy. The company's abuse claims were met with unparalleled global power by dozens of first-hand interviews and rare footage. Then Part 2 explores the reaction of Facebook to the accusations of "fake news" and US politics. This hour demonstrates the international role of the corporation in seeding division and the problems of the strong social media platform.

Last updated on 03/22/2021
More Background

James Jacoby and Anyya Bourg and Dana Priest, Washington Post's winner and journalist and lecturer at Philip Merrill School of Journalism of the University of Maryland, trace how Facebook has failed successfully to respond to both Facebook and external alerts on everything from possible breaches of privacy to "false news." On-camera interviews were conducted by FRONTLINE producers with over a dozen of Facebook employees. The documentary discovers that the company's obsessive pursuit of growth and user information have overshadowed many warnings about the negative effect of the platform on privacy and democracy.

Last updated on 03/22/2021
Part 1 and 2

Facebook pledged to create a more open and connected country. However, the company's inability to protect millions of user information, as well as spread "false news" and disinformation in the United States and around the world, has raised the issue of how real damage has been done to Facebook's success as a social network. The Facebook Dilemma is a deep investigation by the frontline into the social media platform's powerful impact on privacy and democracy in the United States and around the world. This significant two-part FTV event draws on dozens of original interviews and rare pictures to examine a series of Facebook alerts as the company grew from Mark Zuckerberg's Harvard Dorm room into a global empire.
